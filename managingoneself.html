Managing Oneself?

Menstruation is part of the menstrual cycle, which typically lasts about 28 days but can range from 21 to 35 days. The cycle has several phases:

  1. Pads (Sanitary Napkins):

    Pads are a common menstrual product that adheres to underwear to absorb menstrual blood. They come in both disposable and reusable forms, with various sizes and absorbencies to suit different flow levels. Disposable pads need to be changed every 4-6 hours to prevent odor and infection and can generate a significant amount of waste. Reusable pads are more eco-friendly and can be washed and reused multiple times. Pads are easy to use and widely available, making them a popular choice for many.

  2. Tampons:

    Tampons are inserted into the vagina to absorb menstrual blood and come in different absorbencies (regular, super, and super-plus) with or without applicators. They offer discretion and comfort, especially for those with active lifestyles, and help reduce odor. Tampons should be changed every 4-8 hours to prevent Toxic Shock Syndrome (TSS), a rare but serious condition. Proper insertion is important, and some users may experience vaginal dryness with tampons.

  3. Menstrual Cups:

    Menstrual cups are reusable devices made from silicone, rubber, or plastic that are inserted into the vagina to collect menstrual blood. They can be worn for up to 12 hours, making them a convenient option for long-term use. Menstrual cups need to be emptied, washed, and reinserted regularly. They are eco-friendly and cost-effective over time, though there is an initial learning curve for insertion and removal. Proper cleaning is essential to maintain hygiene and prevent infections.
